// Work — seven projects, 2023–2026

Selected work

Seven projects across four ways of working — analysing cities, designing them, researching how they are lived in, and building tools that act on the data. Grouped by practice; strongest first.

Design

01 — Nuova Porta Genova

Masterplan · Milan · 2026

Linear Flow: from infrastructure to urban commons.

A masterplan reimagining the former railway lands of Porta Genova, Milan, as a new urban ecology — a layered civic landscape bringing mobility, housing, culture, water, and biodiversity into one regenerative framework. It grows from a rigorous analytical base — an Urban Green Index built on census and European SPOTTED-portal data, mapping the area's 'green gap,' heat-wave risk, working-class housing, property prices, flooding, and productive heritage across regional, city, and district scales — into a masterplan of six new districts (Corta Studentesca, Oasi Urbana, Passage della Arti, Bosco del Benessere, Corta della Stazione, Corta Creativa) and twenty spatial episodes, adding ~10,000 residents and 7,000 homes (≈309,575 m² residential / 53,624 m² commercial / 38,243 m² civic) while repairing the fragmentation left by the rail corridor.

Context: International Urban Design Studio (HKU, MUDP1002), Milan — Semester II 2026 · Instructor: Ian Ralph · Tools: QGIS · Rhino · Adobe · Proof: full board set

What it demonstrates: analysis driving design — evidence to masterplan at district scale.

02 — Pokfulam Waterfront

Masterplan · Hong Kong · 2025

A waterfront redevelopment masterplan for Pokfulam on Hong Kong Island — opening a continuous public waterfront and weaving student co-living, elderly housing, pocket parks, and residential blocks into the steep coastal slope. A single green-blue promenade threads three activated waterfront zones, with terraced, planted architecture that steps down with the hillside.

Context: Hong Kong Urban Design Studio (HKU, MUD) — Semester I 2025 · Proof: masterplan · aerial · section · renders

What it demonstrates: designing with topography and mixed communities on a real coastline.

03 — Urban Heat Island, Hong Kong

Health impact assessment · 2024

A health-impact assessment built end-to-end from satellite remote sensing.

A health-impact assessment of Hong Kong's urban heat island, built end-to-end from satellite remote sensing. Using summer Landsat 8/9 imagery (C02/T1 L2, Jun–Sep 2023), the analysis derives land-surface temperature and Urban Heat Island Intensity on a 500×500 m grid, then links the thermal burden to built-environment indicators — mean building height, floor-area ratio, and building coverage — showing where heat risk is structured by urban form rather than isolated hotspots.

Tools: QGIS · Python · Landsat remote sensing · Proof: full analytical board

What it demonstrates: end-to-end remote sensing tied to a public-health question.

04 — Space Syntax, Manila

Spatial analysis · 2025

Informal settlements across 2004, 2014, and 2025.

A spatiotemporal space-syntax study of three of Manila's informal settlements — Barangay 176, Bagong Silang, and Payatas — across 2004, 2014, and 2025. Satellite imagery is vectorized in QGIS and analysed for connectivity, intelligibility, and local and global integration, tracing how the structure and legibility of informal urban fabric shift over two decades.

Tools: QGIS · space-syntax analysis · Proof: full analytical board

What it demonstrates: rigorous morphological method applied to under-studied fabric.

05 — Spatial Branding

Place identity · 2023

Staraya Ladoga & Khabarovsk.

Place-identity systems for two Russian territories. 'Staraya Ladoga — a fragment of antiquity' builds a heritage identity from Russian ornament and the legacy of Nicholas Roerich. 'Khabarovsk — Heart of the Far East' is a complete visual system — custom typography, a distinctive palette, and a coat-of-arms mark — applied across transit stops, billboards, and posters.

Tools: InDesign · Photoshop · Figma · Proof: brand boards

What it demonstrates: the designer's hand — type, colour, and identity at city scale.
