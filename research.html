Flagship — How we feel housing

EEG · Master's research, HKU

An EEG study of old vs. new high-density homes.

Master's research, University of Hong Kong (Urban Design). Using EEG (frontal alpha asymmetry) and a controlled, parametric stimulus design, the study measures the affective response to old versus new high-density residential facades — isolating morphological drivers including inhabited visual complexity, weathering, and greenery, with stimuli validated through online ratings (Prolific; SAM / PRS scales) and rigorous statistics (rm-ANOVA, linear mixed models, FDR correction).

Headline finding: the sense of 'home' is culturally tuned, not universal — so 'good' housing can't be designed from a single template.
